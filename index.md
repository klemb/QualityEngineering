[You have found your dream job as a Test Manager or you are wanting to
become a Test Manager. How do you prepare for the start into this new
venture? How do you get to be a very successful Test
Manager?]{.wixui-rich-text__text
style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

 

[The \"Test Manager Startup\" is there to help you! There are six key
areas that will pave the way to success namely Leadership, Vision,
Manage & Organise, People, Process and Tools & Technology.
]{.wixui-rich-text__text
style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

[They key is to put a strategy together that encompasses all of these
areas BEFORE you enter into a new venture and once you are in the thick
of it CONTINOUSLY elaborate and develop these
areas.]{.wixui-rich-text__text
style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

## [[[Leadership]{.wixui-rich-text__text style="color:#292929;"}]{.wixui-rich-text__text style="font-family:avenir-lt-w01_35-light1475496,sans-serif;"}]{.wixui-rich-text__text style="font-size:44px;"} {#leadership .font_2 .wixui-rich-text__text style="font-size:44px;"}

::: {#comp-jt5q4qze .Z_l5lU .ku3DBC .zQ9jDz .qvSjx3 .Vq6kJx .comp-jt5q4qze .wixui-rich-text testid="richTextElement"}
[As you clearly cannot understand and asses every single technical
(domain knowledge & organisational knowledge), you need to set out some
principles. Helping you define these properly is some background
knowledge of \"[The Seven Habits of Highly Effective
People](https://amzn.to/2Aejytv){.wixui-rich-text__text target="_blank"
rel="noreferrer noopener"}\". Those accountable for a process may not
necessarily have the highest competency level. Whilst they may, in the
past, have operated at a higher level of competency - and will draw on
this experience - as a leader, their role has moved beyond \"doing\" to
a \"leadership\" such as setting the culture and environment for the
activity to be done correctly. Of course, if a role involves both
accountability and responsibility, a higher level of competency will
normally be required.]{.wixui-rich-text__text
style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

 

[Moving away from the small problem management also means that there is
more emphasis your leadership qualities. Great inspirations are Steven
Covey\'s \"[The 8th
Habit](https://amzn.to/2R3IrCy){.wixui-rich-text__text target="_blank"
rel="noreferrer noopener"}\", [Simon
Sinek](https://startwithwhy.com/){.wixui-rich-text__text target="_blank"
rel="noreferrer noopener"} and [David Marquet: Turn The Ship
Around!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ivwKQqf4ixA){.wixui-rich-text__text
target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"}.]{.wixui-rich-text__text
style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

[Leadership also means to concentrate on breakthrough improvements to
better the  overall performance of the team. For you to be able to drive
the improvement, at minimum you need to be familiar with
[PDCA](https://amzn.to/2RznlLE){.wixui-rich-text__text target="_blank"
rel="noreferrer noopener"}, better if you have done some training and
certification in [Project](https://www.pmi.org/){.wixui-rich-text__text
target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"} &
[Service](https://amzn.to/2Tnyv3q){.wixui-rich-text__text
target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"} Management. Improvement
Initiatives need to be run as projects, a great tool is \"[The One-Page
Project Manager](https://amzn.to/2StSZrq){.wixui-rich-text__text
target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"}\", and a great bedtime
reading Tom DeMarco\'s \"[The
Deadline](https://amzn.to/2TsETqn){.wixui-rich-text__text
target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"}\"]{.wixui-rich-text__text
style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

[[​]{.wixGuard .wixui-rich-text__text}]{.wixui-rich-text__text
style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

[So before you start on your new endevour, develop a plan. More
specificically a [30-60-90 day
plan](https://www.brendanreid.com/blog-1/2017/9/17/2-ways-to-make-your-30-60-90-day-plan-even-better){.wixui-rich-text__text
target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"}. Many organisations require
you to do this as part of the interview process. So in any case it is
worth preparing for it. The key is to align it to the objectives of the
company (your future boss), list key tasks and deliverables with dates
into three \"buckets\" that are clearly themed, i.e. 30-Understand,
60-Assess, 90-Plan. One of the outcomes needs to be a vision
statement.]{.wixui-rich-text__text
style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

[As a Test Manager, you are dependent on getting a \"testeable\" product
or implementation, so understanding how Project Management works and how
the build process works (Continuous
Integration/Test/Deployment/Delivery), including
[version](https://git-scm.com/){.wixui-rich-text__text target="_blank"
rel="noreferrer noopener"} and configuration control, and being able to
influence this in the right direction, will make your life
easier.]{.wixui-rich-text__text
style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

[Following these key points above should help you to avoid the \"chuck
it over the wall behavior\" and \"blaming\" game when things get
hot.]{.wixui-rich-text__text
style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

# Assign personal responsibility {#assign-personal-responsibility .font_0 .wixui-rich-text__text style="font-size:22px;"}

::: {#comp-jt5tsnj0 .Z_l5lU .ku3DBC .zQ9jDz .qvSjx3 .Vq6kJx .comp-jt5tsnj0 .wixui-rich-text testid="richTextElement"}
[In a similar fashion, make people responsible for certain areas (i.e.
checking automated test results, test environment magament, knowledge
management) and divert any enquiries directly to them before you update
your Stakeholders. This makes your team more visible within the wider
organisation and you don\'t have to micro manage. Just be careful, this
is a gradual process and requires you to build a trusting relationship.
You also need to be clear on your expectations.]{.wixui-rich-text__text

## [[Delegating Simple Problem Solving (Step 4)]{.wixui-rich-text__text style="letter-spacing:normal;"}]{.wixui-rich-text__text style="color:#000000;"} {#delegating-simple-problem-solving-step-4 .font_2 .wixui-rich-text__text style="line-height:normal; font-size:44px;"}

::: {#comp-jt5qbom7 .Z_l5lU .ku3DBC .zQ9jDz .qvSjx3 .Vq6kJx .comp-jt5qbom7 .wixui-rich-text testid="richTextElement"}

[Do not try and solve every problem for your team members, even if you
know how and it would only take you 5 minutes. It is important that your
team members take care of their problems but know that you are there to
help and advise if they get stuck or overwhelmed. You also do not need
to answer every question or respond to every challenge - let teh person

## [[[Process]{.wixui-rich-text__text style="color:#292929;"}]{.wixui-rich-text__text style="font-family:avenir-lt-w01_35-light1475496,sans-serif;"}]{.wixui-rich-text__text style="font-size:44px;"} {#process .font_2 .wixui-rich-text__text style="font-size:44px;"}
:::

::: {#comp-jt5qviz8 .Z_l5lU .ku3DBC .zQ9jDz .qvSjx3 .Vq6kJx .comp-jt5qviz8 .wixui-rich-text testid="richTextElement"}
[Process is important to Testing. Good processes are not a guarante for
great products without them, high quality products are even harder to
achieve. Processes are everywhere. In most cases, the word process is a
tarnished word because it is associated to administration. If you watch
for example
\"[Masterchef](https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b006t1k5){.wixui-rich-text__text
target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"}\", then you will hear the
word process quite a lot. In combination with work, skill, technique and
talent it makes fantastic meals. Without process, i.e. how to beat an
egg properly, those amateur cooks would never be able to produce a
Michelin star like plate of food.]{.wixui-rich-text__text
style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

[[​]{.wixGuard .wixui-rich-text__text}]{.wixui-rich-text__text
style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

[The same applies to the processes used in testing. When they become an
administrative burden, then people will try to avois or shortcut them.
It is essential to define processes appropriate to the organisation and
the type of product or project. Products for medical, public transport
and automotive will have different process requirements then a
Photoeditor.]{.wixui-rich-text__text
style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

[[​]{.wixGuard .wixui-rich-text__text}]{.wixui-rich-text__text
style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

[Process also implies actors. In many cases the diffence between certain
processes is not understood because they are referred to as the same for
example \"Test Automation\" but because the actors are different i.e.
Tester vs. Developer, the process will be different. In these cases it
is helpful to add another dimension, for example Blackbox vs Whitebox
testing, and then define who will do what type of
testing.]{.wixui-rich-text__text
style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

[[​]{.wixGuard .wixui-rich-text__text}]{.wixui-rich-text__text
style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

[Developer tests are concerned with the technical level and work on
smaller units as indicated here in blue. The key metrics that are
tracked for developer tests are number of tests and code coverage
(Whitebox)]{.wixui-rich-text__text
style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

[Automated test are more concerned with requirements coverage and use
code coverage as a quality of test measure. They work on the larger
parts, i.e. what is visible from a customer perspective for example APIs
and User Interface (Blackbox)]{.wixui-rich-text__text
style="font-family:helvetica-w01-roman,helvetica-w02-roman,helvetica-lt-w10-roman,sans-serif;"}

## [[[Tools & Technology]{.wixui-rich-text__text style="color:#292929;"}]{.wixui-rich-text__text style="font-family:avenir-lt-w01_35-light1475496,sans-serif;"}]{.wixui-rich-text__text style="font-size:44px;"} {#tools-technology .font_2 .wixui-rich-text__text style="font-size:44px;"}

[Every craftsman will tell you that using the right tools is half the
job done. So it is in testing. There is little value in \"saving\"
money, just because a tool does have a licence fee attached. In many

also good for attracting and retaining the right
